This project involves the analysis of customer sentiments in Amazon magazine reviews obtained from a Kaggle dataset to classify sentiments as positive, negative, or neutral by comparing the performance of various NLP models. The study aims to identify the most accurate approach for sentiment classification. The methodology involved cleaning and preprocessing the text data to remove noise, tokenizing it into meaningful units, and encoding sentiment labels into numerical form. The models are trained using fine-tuned hyperparameters. Performance evaluation test data shows that BERT outperforms other models like Bi-LSTM, LSTM, and RNN. BERT also demonstrates consistent performance over ten epochs. This methodology can be used for sentiment analysis tasks in various domains, offering a practical solution for businesses to understand and act on customer opinions at scale
