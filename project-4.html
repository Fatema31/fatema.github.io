Overview


Bangladesh is heavily reliant on agriculture as a developing nation. Cultivation of Tomatoes is both economically beneficial and has great benefits health-wise. But due to unawareness and not knowing about the pathogen attacks, our farmers suffer greatly. this project, an efficient way is provided to identify and recognize the diseases that affect the cultivation of tomatoes in Bangladesh. The work was done on a custom dataset.

Here, 500 digital images of tomato leaves, including healthy and unhealthy, were collected from several crop fields of villages of Bangladesh. Then the images were pre-processed by resizing and normalization. Images were augmented for creating the variations. For augmentation on images, several fill_mode like the reflection, wrap, constant and nearest were considered. By augmentation, up to 2500 digital images were created. Then, labels were assigned on those images and encoded was done after that. 30 features combining GLCM, statistical, and color features were extracted. By using the Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) technique, 15 best essential features were selected for leaf disease classification. The custom dataset was split into training and testing set by 80% and 20% ratio. Model was trained using a Random Forest Classifier, Support Vector Machine, and K-Nearest Neighbor algorithms. The models performance was evaluated considering accuracy and F1 score. It was found that Random Forest Classifier shows best result than the other models.


Integrating this system into the backend server of an application, can be used to assist farmers in detecting plant diseases using leaf photos. The user will take a picture of a leaf from an impacted plant with a smartphone or camera that already has the suggested system's application installed. Users will be able to send photos of captured leaves to the proposed model on the backend server. The technology will detect the ailment based on the submitted photograph and deliver its findings to the user.
